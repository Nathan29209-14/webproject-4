Naawi-Oodena

Naawi-Oodena Urban Reserve

  • Meaning: "Naawi-Oodena" means "Centre of the Heart and Community" in the Anishinaabe language.
  • Location: It is in southwest Winnipeg, close to the River Heights and Tuxedo areas.
  • Size: The land is 160 acres, making it the biggest urban reserve in Canada.
  • Leadership: The land is shared and run together by these seven Treaty One First Nations:
    • Brokenhead Ojibway Nation
    • Long Plain First Nation
    • Peguis First Nation
    • Roseau River Anishinaabe First Nation
    • Sagkeeng First Nation
    • Sandy Bay Ojibway First Nation
    • Swan Lake First Nation

Community Features

  • Vision: The goal is to build a strong and welcoming place that respects Indigenous culture and helps the economy grow.
  • Amenities:
    • Different kinds of homes for families, seniors, and individuals.
    • Shops, restaurants, and local services.
    • Lots of green areas like parks and gardens.
    • Schools and cultural centers.
    • Health and wellness services.
    • Places for fun, like sports fields and walking paths.
    • Eco-friendly and accessible public areas.

Housing Options

  • Types of Homes:
    • Houses for one family with yards.
    • Townhouses that are easy to care for.
    • Apartment buildings with different sizes of units.
    • Affordable homes for all income levels.
    • Seniors homes with special features for easier living.
  • Design Philosophy: Homes will look good next to other neighborhoods and will include Indigenous design and eco-friendly building ideas.

Business & Shopping

  • Commercial Spaces:
    • Grocery stores and food markets.
    • Places to eat like restaurants and cafes.
    • Shops and services.
    • Stores owned by Indigenous people.
    • Office space and a business center for the community.
    • Tourist spots focused on Indigenous culture.
  • Economic Impact: These businesses will give people jobs and provide things the community needs.

Parks & Recreation

  • Outdoor Spaces:
    • Parks with play areas for kids.
    • Community gardens and green paths.
    • Sports fields and courts.
    • Walking and biking trails.
    • Places for cultural events and gatherings.
  • Recreation Facilities:
    • Community center with programs and events.
    • Spaces for youth activities.
    • Fitness and health centers.
    • Programs for learning about Indigenous culture.
    • Rooms for meetings and community events.
  • Purpose: These places are made to improve peoples lives by giving them areas to relax, play, and connect with others.

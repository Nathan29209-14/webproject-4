Naawi-Oodena

Naawi-Oodena is a large urban reserve in Winnipeg, Manitoba, developed by seven First Nations: Brokenhead, Long Plain, Peguis, Roseau River, Sagkeeng, Sandy Bay, and Swan Lake. The name "Naawi-Oodena" means "centre of the heart and community" in the Anishinaabe language. The land was once used by the Canadian military but was officially returned to the First Nations in 2022 after a long legal process. This makes Naawi-Oodena the largest urban reserve in Canada. Plans for Naawi-Oodena include building homes, businesses, schools, parks, and health centres over the next 15 years. The goal is to create jobs, support local businesses, and provide services for both Indigenous and non-Indigenous people in Winnipeg. The development is a significant step toward reconciliation and self-determination for Indigenous communities.

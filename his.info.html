Historical info

Naawi-Oodena

Origins and Significance

  • Treaty One: Signed on August 3, 1871, at Lower Fort Garry, Treaty One was the first of the numbered treaties in Canada. It involved the Anishinaabe and Swampy Cree peoples and the Canadian Crown.
  • Land Promises: The treaty promised each family of five 160 acres of land in exchange for ceding large portions of southern Manitoba to the Crown.

Kapyong Barracks Site

  • Military Use: The land now known as Naawi-Oodena was occupied by the Canadian military's Princess Patricia's Canadian Light Infantry until 2004.
  • Legal Challenges: After the military vacated the site, the federal government attempted to sell the land without consulting the Treaty One First Nations, leading to legal disputes.
  • Court Rulings: In 2009, a Federal Court judge blocked the government's plans due to lack of consultation. The government lost subsequent appeals, and in 2015, it ceased further legal action.

Repatriation and Development

  • Land Transfer: On August 30, 2019, a settlement agreement was signed to transfer the land to the seven Treaty One First Nations.
  • Official Repatriation: The land was officially converted to reserve status and repatriated on December 16, 2022, becoming the largest urban reserve in Canada.
  • Municipal Agreement: On July 20, 2022, the Treaty One First Nations and the City of Winnipeg signed the Gaawijijigemangit Agreement, a Municipal Development & Services Agreement, to provide city services to Naawi-Oodena. "Gaawijijigemangit" means "Working Together in Partnership" in Ojibway.

Cultural Recognition

  • Naming Ceremony: The name "Naawi-Oodena" was given through a ceremony by the late Dr. Dave Courchene Jr., a respected Knowledge Keeper from Sagkeeng First Nation.
  • City Honors: During the signing ceremony of the Gaawijijigemangit Agreement, the City of Winnipeg presented Keys to the City to the Chiefs of the seven Treaty One First Nations, recognizing their leadership and the significance of the development.

Future Outlook

  • Economic Potential: Naawi-Oodena is envisioned as a landmark development that will generate significant economic investment, shape Winnipeg's urban growth, and serve as a model for future urban reserve developments across Canada.

Historical

"Treaty 1 was signed on August 3, 1871, by the Canadian government and seven Indigenous nations in southern Manitoba. The treaty allowed the government to use the land, but in exchange, the Indigenous people were promised land reserves, money, and goods. However, the Canadian government didn't keep many of these promises, which caused problems for the First Nations. They lost land and resources, and these issues still affect them today. Because of this, there are ongoing efforts to fix past wrongs, get back land, and make things right through reconciliation."
